NATIONAL SOLAR RADIATION DATABASE

ABOUT

WHAT IS NSRDB

The NSRDB is a serially complete collection of hourly and half-hourly values of the three most common measurements of solar radiation—global horizontal, direct normal, and diffuse horizontal irradiance—and meteorological data.

  • U.S. DATA
  • INTERNATIONAL DATA
  • TYPICAL METEOROLOGICAL YEAR

ABOUT THE DATA

Using these data, it's possible to see the amount of solar energy that was available at a given time and location anywhere in the United States; the NSRDB is also expanding to encompass a growing list of internationallocations. Using the data in models, it's possible to predict the potential future availability of solar energy in a location based on past conditions. The primary type of data derived from the NSRDB is called Typical Meteorological Year (TMY). Visit the TMY pagefor detailed information about this data type and its uses.

COMPATIBILITY

The NSRDB and TMY data are compatible with many system performance and economic models, including the following products created by NREL

  • SYSTEM ADVISOR MODEL
  • PVWATTS
  • SOLAR INTEGRATION NATIONAL DATASET
